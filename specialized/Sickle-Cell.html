Sickle Cell

sickle cell services are dedicated to the comprehensive care and management of sickle cell disease (SCD), a genetic blood disorder. Patients with SCD receive specialized diagnosis through genetic testing and monitoring of hemoglobin levels. Treatment plans are tailored to alleviate symptoms and complications associated with the disease, which include pain crises, anemia, and organ damage. The hospital provides ongoing medical care, including pain management, blood transfusions when necessary, and medications to prevent infections and complications. Education and counseling are also integral parts of the service, aimed at empowering patients and their families to understand and manage the challenges of living with sickle cell disease. Tema General Hospital's sickle cell services focus on improving quality of life and reducing complications through comprehensive care, support, and education.
